Miso Ramen

Omurice is a beloved Japanese-Western fusion dish featuring ketchup-seasoned fried rice (usually with chicken and veggies) wrapped in a soft, fluffy omelette.

Ingredients

  • Cooked Japanese short-grain rice (about 2 cups, preferably day-old or cooled)
  • Chicken thigh or breast, diced (about 100-150g)
  • Onion, finely chopped (1/2 medium)
  • Carrot, finely diced (optional, 1/4 cup)
  • Green peas or mixed veggies (optional, 1/4 cup)
  • Ketchup (4-6 tbsp, plus extra for topping)
  • Butter or oil (for frying)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Large eggs (2-3 per serving, beaten with a pinch of salt)

Steps

  1. Make the fried rice: Heat butter or oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté diced chicken until cooked, add chopped onion (and carrot/peas if using), and cook until softened.
  2. Add the rice: Stir in the cooked rice, breaking up clumps. Pour in ketchup (and soy/Worcestershire if using), mix well until the rice is evenly coated and heated through. Season with salt/pepper. Set aside and keep warm.
  3. Prepare the omelette: Beat eggs with a pinch of salt. Heat a non-stick pan with a little oil or butter over medium-low heat. Pour in the eggs and gently swirl to make a thin, even layer. Cook until softly set (still slightly runny on top for fluffiness).
  4. Serve: Slide or flip onto a plate so the smooth side is up. Drizzle with extra ketchup in a zigzag pattern (and mayo if desired). Cut open at the table to reveal the rice inside—enjoy hot!
